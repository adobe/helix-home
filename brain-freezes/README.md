# SLICC Brain Freezes

Brain Freezes are the gatherings of the SLICC (Self-Licking Ice Cream Cone) crowd. They were forked off the
Helix Operations Workshops so the ice-cream truck has its own room (and its own sugar rush): same hands-on,
code-settles-arguments energy as a hackathon, just with more sprinkles and the occasional headache from eating
progress too fast.

If you mostly show up to a Helix event to hack on SLICC, this is your track.

## What's a Brain Freeze?

> Is this just a hackathon with a sillier name?

Pretty much. A Brain Freeze is a short, focused sprint on SLICC and the things around it — the browser-native
agent runtime, the shell, skills, browser automation, the Mac app and extension, sliccy.ai, and whatever weird
in-between work doesn't fit into a 45-minute Teams meeting. We keep it hands-on: instead of presentations, we let
code settle arguments.

The main purpose is the same as any hackathon: reconnect, onboard new contributors, and move fast on things that
need a room and a whiteboard rather than a thread.

## Let's do it!

> How to organize a Brain Freeze

1. Find a couple of days where the SLICC core can be in one place (co-locating with a Helix Operations Workshop works well)
2. Book a **separate** room — Brain Freezers and Operators are happier when they're not fighting over the same whiteboard
3. Wifi, power, and a freezer stocked with ice cream
4. Copy one of the existing Brain Freeze pages
5. Put your name on the attendee list
